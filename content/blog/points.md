Recently, I’ve become intrigued by how the paths we choose in life shape both our self-perception and how others perceive us. The book that sparked this interest for me was Surrounded by Idiots. Its main goal is to help readers understand why some people behave in ways that may seem irrational from our perspective. A great example is the contrast between entrepreneurs and those who prefer a traditional 9-to-5 job. It’s not about one path being better than the other—it’s about recognizing that people have different values, priorities, and perspectives on life.

I once heard a well-known entrepreneur say on his podcast that life comes down to about 30 major forks in the road. You choose whether to go to university or start working, spend time with one person or another, and so on. Looking back, I realize that I might not be where I am today if it weren’t for my chess mentor. One of the most valuable lessons he taught me was the habit of reading books to sharpen my chess skills. That habit has become one of my favorite traits—being curious about something and diving deep into research and learning.

Maybe it's because we're not fully conscious of it as kids, but we often find heroes among adults—people who inspire us through the work they do or the passion they show. Often, the paths we choose in life are sparked by someone who lit the way for us.

A truly beautiful way to see life is not just about connecting the dots, but about becoming one of those dots yourself—someone who inspires others, who shines a bit of light for those still searching for direction.
